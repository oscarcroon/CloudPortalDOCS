# DNS records

DNS records control how your domain works — where web traffic and email point, how services are verified and more. You manage records inside a zone. First open the zone from [DNS zones](/en/domains-and-dns/dns-zones).

## Find the right records

On the zone page the records are shown in a table with the columns **Type**, **Name**, **Content**, **TTL** and **Actions**.

- Use the **search field** to search the records.
- Use the **type filter** to show only a specific record type (or **All**).

## Create a record

1. Open the zone and click **Add record**.
2. Select the **Type** (e.g. A, AAAA, CNAME, MX, TXT).
3. Fill in the **Name**. Use `@` for the zone root (apex) or a prefix such as `www` for a subdomain.
4. Fill in the **Content** / value. The fields adapt to the record type:
   - **A** — an IPv4 address, e.g. `192.168.1.1`
   - **AAAA** — an IPv6 address, e.g. `2001:db8::1`
   - **CNAME** — a target, e.g. `target.example.com`
   - **MX** — mail server plus **Priority**
   - **TXT** — a text value, e.g. an SPF line
5. Set the **TTL** (how long the answer may be cached).
6. Optionally add a **Comment** — an internal note that is not visible in DNS.
7. Review the **Preview**, which shows the record that will be created.
8. Click **Save**.

::tip
Some record types have extra fields. MX has **Priority**, SRV has **Weight**, **Port** and **Target** among others, and CAA has **Flags**, **Tag** and **Value**. The form shows the fields that apply to the selected type.
::

## Edit and delete records

- Click **Edit** on a record to change its values, then **Save**.
- Click **Delete** to remove the record. You will be asked to confirm the deletion.

## Rules to be aware of

Some combinations are not allowed in DNS, and the portal will then show a clear error:

- A **CNAME** cannot live at the zone root (`@`). Use an A or AAAA record instead, or a name such as `www`.
- A **CNAME** must be alone on its name — it cannot coexist with other records on the same name.
- **NS records at the zone root** are managed automatically and cannot be created or changed through the portal. You can create NS records on subdomains.

::note
The SOA record and the zone's name servers are shown in their own panels and are not edited in the records table — they are managed automatically.
::

## Records managed by other features

Some records are marked as managed by another feature and should not be changed manually:

- **Redirect** — the record is managed by the redirect service. See [Redirects](/en/domains-and-dns/redirects).
- **MTA-STS** — the record is managed by the email security module.

::warning
If you change a record that is managed by another feature, it can affect that feature. The portal warns you before you edit such a record.
::

## Change history

For records with history you can see when the record was last changed and by whom. If there is no history, "No change history" is shown.

## Downstream effects

::note
The portal publishes your DNS records, but how quickly a change takes effect is determined by caching (TTL) at other DNS servers and resolvers along the way. Expect that changes can take time to propagate everywhere.
::
