# Support flows

Support flows help you keep customers informed. You publish incidents and operational notices, share news, and control which messages are shown to your customers. Everything is reached from the partner section.

## Incidents and operational notices

Incidents are used to inform about disruptions, planned maintenance and similar events.

### Create an incident

1. Go to **Incidents** in the partner section, or use the Incidents tab on the partner home page.
2. Click **Create incident**.
3. Enter a **title**.
4. Choose a **severity** — for example critical, disruption, maintenance, planned or notice.
5. Optionally set a **start time** and **end time**. An incident with a future start time is shown as scheduled.
6. Write a **description** in the text field. You can format the text with Markdown and preview the result.
7. Click **Publish**.

::tip
Set a future start time when you plan maintenance in advance. The incident is then marked as scheduled until it takes effect.
::

### Follow up on incidents

On the partner home page and in the incident list you can filter by all, ongoing, scheduled and resolved incidents.

- An active incident can be marked as **resolved** when it is completed.
- Resolved incidents are shown with a clear status.
- Click the pencil to open and edit an incident.

## News

With news you share information that is not operationally related, for example updates and tips.

### Create a news post

1. Go to **News** in the partner section, or use the News tab on the partner home page.
2. Click **Create news**.
3. Enter a **title** and optionally a short **summary**.
4. Write the **content** with Markdown and preview if needed.
5. Optionally add an **image** and set a **slug** (URL part) for the post.
6. Save. The post is created as a draft.

A post can have the status draft, published or archived. Click **Edit** in the news list to open and change a post.

::

## Visibility

Visibility controls which incoming incidents your customers actually see. You can receive messages from upstream levels and choose to hide individual ones for a tenant.

1. Go to **Visibility** in the partner section.
2. Under **active incidents** you see the messages you receive, with source and severity.
3. Click **Hide for tenant** to stop showing a particular incident for the tenant you are in.
4. Hidden incidents are listed separately. Click **Show for tenant** to display them again.

::note
Hiding an incident affects the display for the tenant you are in. It does not remove the incident itself at the source.
::

::tip
Use visibility to filter out messages that are not relevant to a specific customer, so that customers only see what concerns them.
::
