# Roles and permissions

This page describes how you control what your team is allowed to do at customers. You work with members and roles at the tenant level, with MSP roles that describe which module permissions the team gets, and with delegations for temporary access.

## Members and roles

Every tenant has members with a role that determines the base level of access.

1. Go to a tenant's **Members**.
2. You see a list of members with name, email, role and status.
3. Use **Invite member** to add new people via email.
4. Change a member's role directly in the list when needed.

Pending invitations are shown separately. There you can resend or cancel an invitation.

::warning
You cannot downgrade your own admin privileges. That change must be made by another administrator.
::

## MSP roles

MSP roles are used for staff who will work for multiple customer organizations. An MSP role describes which module permissions the role grants, and is created per provider.

### Create and manage MSP roles

1. Go to the tenant's **MSP roles**.
2. Click **Create MSP role**.
3. Give the role a **name** and a **description**.
4. Choose which **permissions** the role should grant. The permissions come from the installed modules, and you can search among modules and permissions.
5. Save the role.

You can also **edit**, **clone** and **delete** existing roles. The list shows how many members each role is used by.

::note
MSP roles are not predefined — you build them up as needed. This lets you grant exactly the access your team needs, no more and no less.
::

### MSP role templates (as a distributor)

As a distributor you can offer ready-made **MSP role templates** to your providers. A provider can then create a role based on a template instead of starting from scratch.

- Go to the tenant's **MSP role templates** to manage the template library.
- In a provider's MSP roles view, you can choose **Create from template**.

::tip
If a provider sees no templates, the template library is empty. As a distributor you fill it; as a provider you contact your distributor.
::

## Access to all organizations

For a member with an MSP role, you can control how broadly access applies:

- **Specific organizations** — choose exactly which customers the member should reach.
- **All organizations** — the member gets access to all customers under the provider via their MSP role.

::warning
Only grant access to all organizations to trusted administrators. All customers are then shown in the context switcher. The setting has no effect without an active MSP role.
::

## Delegations

With delegations you can give a person permissions in a single organization without adding the person as a member there. This suits temporary assignments.

1. Open the organization's **Delegations**.
2. Search for and select the user.
3. Choose which **permissions** the delegation should grant — search among modules and permissions.
4. Optionally set a **valid until** date and a **comment**.
5. Click **Create delegation**.

Revoke a delegation with **Revoke** when it is no longer needed. You can view revoked and expired delegations for history.

::note
If a user has multiple active delegations, the permissions are combined. This may be intentional, but verify that the combined access is what you want to grant.
::
