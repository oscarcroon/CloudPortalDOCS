# Tenants and organizations

In the partner section you administer the structure of tenants and customer organizations you are responsible for. This page describes how the structure fits together and how you work with it in the portal.

## How the structure fits together

The partner structure has three levels:

- **Distributor** — the top partner level. A distributor can create and manage multiple providers.
- **Provider** — an MSP partner that delivers services to end customers. A provider creates and manages customer organizations.
- **Organization** — an end customer. Organizations always belong to a provider.

Which actions you see in the portal depend on the type of tenant you are logged in to. As a distributor you work mainly with providers; as a provider you work mainly with organizations.

::note
If you manage multiple tenants, you switch between them using the context switcher. The tenant you are in determines which customers and actions are shown.
::

## Find your partner overview

1. Open the partner section in the portal.
2. On the home page you see the tenant you are in, with quick statistics on the number of organizations (or providers), members and active modules.
3. Click a number to go directly to the corresponding list.
4. Under **Tenant management** you find quick links to members, email settings, modules, branding, login domain, audit logs and authentication.

::tip
Click **View details** to open the full view for the current tenant, or **Tenant tree** to see the entire structure.
::

## Manage providers (as a distributor)

As a distributor you see your providers in the provider list.

1. Go to **Providers** in the partner section.
2. Search by name and filter by status (active, inactive or all).
3. Click a provider to open its detail view.
4. Use **Archive** or **Restore** to deactivate or reactivate a provider.

How to create new providers is described in [Customer assignment](/en/partner/customer-assignment).

## Manage organizations (as a provider)

As a provider you see your customer organizations in the organization list.

1. Go to **Organizations** in the partner section.
2. Search by name or identifier to find the right customer.
3. Each row shows the number of members and the status (active or inactive).
4. Click an organization to open its overview, where you can reach members, email and delegations, among other things.

::note
The list shows the organizations that belong to the tenant you are in. If the list is empty, you may need to switch to the correct provider first.
::

## Tenant settings

From a tenant's detail view you can access settings that affect how the customer experiences the portal:

- **Members** — which people have access and with which role. See [Roles and permissions](/en/partner/roles-and-permissions).
- **Modules** — which features are enabled.
- **Email settings** — sender and email configuration.
- **Branding** — customized appearance.
- **Login domain** — a dedicated domain for login.
- **Audit logs** — review activity in the tenant and its organizations.
- **Authentication** — SSO and login rules.

::warning
Some settings, such as a dedicated login domain, require DNS to be repointed. The portal can publish and verify what lies within the portal, but the final effect depends on external DNS servers and providers. Expect that it may take time before changes take effect everywhere.
::
