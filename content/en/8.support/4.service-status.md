# Service status

Here you can see whether anything is going on right now – service disruptions, maintenance, and other important information. Service status is shown directly in the portal.

## Where to find service status

1. Open **Service status** in the portal (also linked as **Status page** in the top menu).
2. The page shows **active service disruptions** and the **latest news** from your providers and distributors.
3. Click a message to see details.

::note
Screenshot: the Service status page with the list of active service disruptions and latest news.
::

## Understanding a service message

Each service message has a severity and a status.

### Severity

- **Critical** – serious impact.
- **Service disruption** – the service is not working as it should.
- **Maintenance** – ongoing or planned maintenance.
- **Information** – an advisory with no direct service impact.
- **Planned** – a planned action, often with a start and end time.

### Status

- **Active** – the message is ongoing.
- **Resolved** – the problem has been fixed.
- **Closed** – the matter is concluded.

In the detail view you also see times: when the message **starts**, **ends**, and was **resolved**, as well as which provider or distributor it comes from.

## Planned maintenance

Planned maintenance is published in advance as a service message, often with severity **Maintenance** or **Planned** and a specified start and end time. Keep an eye on the service status page ahead of announced times.

## Notifications

You are alerted to service messages and news via notifications in the portal.

- Open the notification panel to see the latest **service messages** and **news**.
- You can mark notifications as read.

::tip
You can hide a service message that isn't relevant to you and show it again later via "Show again" in the service message settings.
::

## When the information isn't enough

If you experience a problem that isn't listed among the active service disruptions:

1. Read [Errors and troubleshooting](/en/support/errors-and-troubleshooting) – it may be something you can resolve yourself.
2. If the problem persists, go to [Contact support](/en/support/contact-support).

::warning
Service status reflects what providers and distributors have published in the portal. An individual service can be affected before a message has been posted – please report it to support if you see a disruption that hasn't been announced.
::
