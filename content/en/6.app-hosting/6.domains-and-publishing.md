# Domains and publishing

Once your app is built and published, you can connect a custom domain to it. Domain management is found on the app's page in App Hosting.

::note
A published app is always reachable via its automatic Live URL. A custom domain is an addition on top of that.
::

## Add a domain

1. Open the app under **My apps**.
2. Go to **Domains**.
3. Click **Add domain**.
4. Enter the **domain name** (for example `app.yourcompany.com`).
5. Save.

The domain is added as an intent. The portal begins the configuration and shows status while DNS and certificates take effect.

## Status for domain, DNS and certificate

For each domain, status is shown in three parts:

- **Domain** — the overall state: Waiting for configuration, Waiting for DNS, Waiting for certificate, Ready or Failed.
- **DNS** — whether the DNS record is requested, in progress, created, verified or failed.
- **Certificate** — whether the certificate is requested, in progress, issued or failed.

Click **Refresh status** to fetch the latest state.

::note
Screenshot: the domain list with status labels for DNS and certificate.
::

## How publishing works downstream

Adding a domain in the portal registers an intent. DNS records and certificates are handled by the portal's DNS and certificate functions and can take time to take effect.

::warning
The portal can publish and verify the DNS record it manages, but how quickly a change appears depends on DNS propagation and caching outside the portal. Expect that it may take a while before a new domain is fully active.
::

If a domain's DNS is managed outside the portal, you may need to add the records the portal specifies at your DNS provider before verification and certificates can be completed.

## Health checks

When the app is running, you can add **health checks** that monitor whether a given URL responds with the expected status. This helps you confirm that the domain points correctly and that the app is live.
