# Roles and permissions

Every member of the organization has a **role**. The role determines what the person can see and do. You assign roles on the [Members](/en/organization/members) page.

## The roles at a glance

| Role | What the role can normally do |
| --- | --- |
| **Owner** | Full control over the organization, including members, settings and billing. |
| **Administrator** | Manages the organization and its members, but does not have the owner's billing responsibility. |
| **Operator** | Works in the organization and can see the member list, but does not manage members or settings. |
| **Member** | Standard role with access to the organization's content. |
| **Viewer** | Read access — can see but not change. |
| **Support** | Visibility role for support purposes, including reading audit logs. |

::note
Exactly what each role can do also depends on which modules and features are enabled in your particular organization. The table describes the typical outcome.
::

## How to assign a role

1. Go to [Members](/en/organization/members).
2. Find the person in the list.
3. Select the role from the drop-down menu in the **Role** column.

The change is saved immediately.

::tip
Give each person the lowest role that is sufficient for their work. It is easier to raise permissions later than to untangle overly broad rights.
::

## Custom permissions per module

In addition to the base role, you can fine-tune what a member is allowed to do in individual modules. You do this via the **Permissions** button on the member's row, which opens a panel listing the modules.

For each permission you can choose:

- **Allow** — give the member the specific permission.
- **Deny** — block the permission even if the role would normally grant it.
- **Inherit** — follow what the role grants by default (no specific setting).

You can also quickly set **Allow all**, **Deny all** or **Reset (inherit)** for an entire module.

::note
Some permissions may be marked as *Blocked by policy*. This means the organization's settings prevent the permission, and in that case it cannot be allowed for individual members.
::

When you make changes they are marked as **Unsaved**. Click **Save** for each module, or **Save all** to save everything at once. Members who have custom permissions get a marker in the member list and can be filtered there.

::warning
Custom permissions sit on top of the role and can be easy to forget. Use them sparingly and review them regularly, so that access stays predictable.
::
