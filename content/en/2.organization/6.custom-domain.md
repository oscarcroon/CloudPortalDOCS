# Custom domain

You can let your organization reach the portal on your **own domain**, for example `portal.company.com`, instead of the default address. The domain gets an SSL certificate automatically. You configure it under **Settings → Domain**.

::note
Partners do the same for a customer under **Partner → Tenants → [customer] → Domain**.
::

## Step 1 — Enter the domain

1. Go to **Settings → Domain**.
2. Type the domain (e.g. `portal.company.com`) and click **Save**.
3. The portal now shows the DNS records to add at your DNS provider.

## Step 2 — Add the DNS records

The portal shows two records (with copy buttons):

**CNAME** — points traffic to the portal:
| Type | Name | Value |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `CNAME` | `portal.company.com` (your domain) | `portal.coreit.network` |

**TXT** — verifies that you own the domain:
| Type | Name | Value |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `TXT` | `_cloudportal-verify.portal.company.com` | `cp-verify=…` (the value the portal shows) |

## Step 3 — Verify and wait for activation

1. Once DNS has propagated, click **Verify**. (The portal also checks automatically at intervals.)
2. The status moves through: **Pending → Verified → Provisioning queued → Awaiting certificate → Active**.
3. When the status is **Active**, the domain is live with valid SSL.

::warning
DNS changes can take time to propagate. A **Moved** status means the CNAME no longer points correctly; **Error** shows a reason. Check the records against what the portal displays.
::

## Branding on the sign-in page

When the domain is **Active**, your [branding](/en/organization/branding) is applied automatically on the sign-in page for that domain.
