# Members

On the **Members** page you can see everyone who has access to your organization, what role they have and whether they are active. This is where you invite new people, change their role and remove access.

::note
To invite or manage members you need the appropriate permission. If you lack permission, an information box is shown and the buttons are disabled. Ask an administrator or owner for help.
::

## Overview of the member list

For each member, the list shows:

- **Name** and sign-in email.
- **Role** — controls what the person is allowed to do (see [Roles and permissions](/en/organization/roles-and-permissions)).
- **Status** — Active, Invited or Inactive.
- **Last updated** — when the member's details were last changed.

At the top there is a summary, for example how many members are active, invited or have pending invitations.

::tip
Use the search field to quickly find a person by name or email. You can also tick **Show only members with manual permissions** to see who has custom permissions in addition to their role.
::

Some members may have a small marker indicating that their account comes from an automatic connection (for example via your company's sign-in system) or that they have manually customized permissions.

## Inviting a member

1. Click **Invite member** in the top right.
2. Enter the person's **email address**.
3. Choose a **role** for the member.
4. Click **Send invitation**.

The person receives an email with a link to join. The invitation appears under **Invitations** with the status *Pending* until it is accepted.

::note
In some organizations you can choose to activate the member directly without an email link. That option only appears when the organization requires single sign-on (SSO).
::

### Managing pending invitations

Under **Invitations**, for a pending invitation you can:

- **Resend** — if the email did not arrive.
- **Cancel** — to withdraw the invitation before it has been accepted.

Invitations have an expiration date. Once it has passed, you must send a new invitation.

## Changing a member's role

1. Find the member in the list.
2. Select a new role from the drop-down menu in the **Role** column.

The change is saved immediately. Only active members can change role.

::warning
Be careful when lowering your own permissions. If a change would remove capabilities you need, a confirmation box appears describing what you will lose. Make sure someone else retains full administrative access before you confirm.
::

## Suspending or reactivating access

- **Deactivate** — pauses an active member's access without deleting the account. The person can no longer sign in to the organization.
- **Activate** — restores access for an inactive member.

This is useful for things like parental leave or temporary assignments.

## Removing a member

1. Click **Remove** on the member's row.
2. Confirm the action.

The member then loses their access to the organization.

::warning
If the member comes from your company's automatic account synchronization, the person may be added back automatically at the next sync. In that case, disable access in your sign-in system to prevent it.
::
