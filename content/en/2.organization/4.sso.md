# Single sign-on (SSO)

With SSO, your users sign in to CloudPortal with your existing organization account — for example **Microsoft Entra ID** or a generic **OpenID Connect (OIDC)** provider. You configure it under **Settings → Authentication**.

::note
Are you an MSP partner configuring this for a customer? You do the same under **Partner → Tenants → [customer] → Authentication**.
::

## Before you start

1. **Verify at least one domain.** Under Authentication, add your email domain (e.g. `company.com`) and you'll get a **TXT record** to publish in DNS:
   - Type: `TXT`
   - Name: `_cloudportal-verify.<your-domain>`
   - Value: the verification string the portal shows

   Publish the record and click **Verify**. The status changes from *Pending* to *Verified*. At least one verified domain is required before you can add an identity provider.

## Add an identity provider

1. Click **Add IdP**.
2. Choose the provider type: **Entra** or **Generic OIDC**.
3. Enter a **Key** (short id, e.g. `entra-prod`) and an optional **display name**.
4. Fill in the details from your identity provider:

   **Microsoft Entra ID**
   - Tenant ID
   - Client ID
   - Client Secret
   - Scopes (default: `openid,email,profile,offline_access`)

   **Generic OIDC**
   - Issuer (e.g. `https://idp.example.com`)
   - Metadata URL (optional)
   - Client ID
   - Client Secret
   - Scopes (default: `openid,email,profile`)

5. The portal shows a **Redirect URI** (read-only) to register in your identity provider:
   ```
   https://<your-domain>/api/auth/sso/<key>/callback
   ```
   Add it as an allowed redirect/callback in Entra or your OIDC provider.

6. Save. Enable the provider with its **on/off toggle**. The status shows *Active* when the configuration is valid.

## Require SSO (optional)

Once at least one domain is verified and a valid IdP is active, you can tick **Require SSO** — then all users must sign in via your identity provider.

::tip
Tick **Allow local login for owners** to keep password sign-in as a fallback for owners, even when SSO is required.
::

## Custom domain and SSO

If you use a [custom domain](/en/organization/custom-domain) for the portal, SSO still works: the user is sent to your identity provider and back to the domain they started from. You only need to register **one** redirect URI per environment with your provider.
