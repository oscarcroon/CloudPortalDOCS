# MTA-STS

MTA-STS lets you require that other mail servers use TLS (encryption) when delivering email to your domain — and that they only accept your genuine MX hosts. It protects against eavesdropping and misrouted delivery if the DNS response is tampered with.

The portal hosts your policy file over HTTPS and creates and maintains the required DNS records, so you don't have to run your own web server.

## Prerequisites

- **The domain is verified.** Domains that already exist in your DNS module are approved automatically. Other domains need to be verified via a TXT challenge (see below).
- **At least one MX host** to include in the policy — usually the same hosts as your active MX records.
- **Optional:** a `mailto:` or `https:` address for TLS-RPT reports, which receives reports when TLS delivery fails.

## How to create a policy

1. Go to **Email security → MTA-STS** and click **New policy**.
2. Enter the **domain**. Domains from your DNS module are suggested automatically in the field. Once you've entered a valid domain, the portal checks whether MTA-STS records or an existing policy already exist and shows that.
3. Choose a **mode**:
   - **Testing** — failures are reported but delivery is allowed. Use this first to phase in the policy.
   - **Enforce** — sending mail servers refuse delivery when the policy can't be satisfied.
4. Add your **MX hosts** (e.g. `mx1.example.com`). At least one is required.
5. Set the **Max age** (in seconds) — how long recipients may cache the policy.
6. Enable **TLS-RPT reporting** if needed and provide a `mailto:` or `https:` address.
7. Click **Create draft**.

The policy is created as a draft. It is not published to DNS until you choose to publish it.

::tip
Always start in **Testing** mode. Verify that mail flow works with TLS end to end before you switch to **Enforce**.
::

### Verify domain ownership

If the domain doesn't come from your DNS module, you need to prove that you own it before the policy can be published:

1. Open the policy and find the domain ownership section.
2. Add the displayed **TXT record** to the domain's DNS.
3. Click **Verify now**.

Domains that match a DNS zone you have access to are approved automatically and revalidated at publish time.

## Publish the policy

Once the policy looks right, you publish it. This creates or updates three DNS records in your zone: a discovery record, a record that points to the portal's policy hosting, and (if you enabled TLS-RPT) a reporting record.

1. Open the policy and click **Publish**.
2. Review the records that will be applied.
3. If conflicting records already exist, a conflict is shown — confirm that you want to **take over existing records** to rewrite them according to the policy.

When the policy is created directly from a domain with existing records, you can also choose **Create and take over existing records** to create and publish in one step.

::note
The portal's policy hosting is enabled automatically at publish time. Preparing the SSL certificate can take up to roughly 15 minutes — in the meantime a status message indicates that hosting is being prepared.
::

::warning
The portal publishes the DNS records and hosts the policy file, but receiving mail servers decide for themselves whether to honor the policy. "Published" means the records exist in DNS — not that every recipient respects them.
::

## Status and drift

Each policy shows a status, for example **Draft**, **Published** or **Out of sync**.

If someone changes or removes the managed DNS records manually, the policy is marked **Out of sync** and a banner appears. Click **Publish** to restore the records, or **Unpublish** if the policy is no longer in use.

## Unpublish a policy

Unpublishing removes all MTA-STS and TLS-RPT records for the domain from DNS.

::warning
Unpublishing in **Enforce** mode is risky. Mail servers may keep enforcing TLS for up to `max_age` seconds after the records are removed, which can break delivery without letting you recover quickly. Recommendation: first switch to **Testing** with a low `max_age`, wait until the cache expires, and then unpublish.
::

To unpublish, open the policy, click **Unpublish** and confirm by typing the domain name.
