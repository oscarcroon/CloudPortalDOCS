# BIMI

BIMI (Brand Indicators for Message Identification) publishes a DNS record that points recipients to your brand logo and an optional Mark Certificate. Some recipients — such as Gmail, Yahoo and Apple — can then display your logo next to authenticated email.

The portal creates and maintains the BIMI record for you, verifies that your logo URL is reachable and checks that DMARC is in enforcement.

::warning
Publishing the BIMI record is necessary but not sufficient. Recipients also require DMARC in enforcement and often a Mark Certificate before the logo is displayed. The portal can publish the record, but cannot guarantee that recipients will display the logo.
::

## Prerequisites

- **DMARC in enforcement.** The DMARC policy must be `p=quarantine` or `p=reject` with `pct=100` for major recipients to display the logo.
- **An HTTPS-hosted SVG logo.** The BIMI SVG Tiny PS profile is recommended. You can either provide an external HTTPS URL or upload an SVG file that the portal hosts.
- **Optional but strongly recommended:** a Mark Certificate (VMC or CMC) in PEM format. Some recipients (e.g. Gmail) require it.

## How to create a BIMI policy

1. Go to **Email security → BIMI** and click **New BIMI policy**.
2. Enter the **domain**. Domains from your DNS module are approved automatically; other domains require a TXT verification record. At the same time, the portal checks whether a BIMI policy or an existing BIMI record already exists for the domain.
3. Configure the **brand logo** (required). Choose one of two modes:
   - **Upload (portal hosts)** — select an SVG file. The portal stores and serves it. Max 128 KiB, recommended under 32 KiB.
   - **External URL** — provide an HTTPS URL to an SVG you host yourself (e.g. from DigiCert or Entrust).
4. Configure a **Mark Certificate** if needed (optional). Choose **No certificate**, **Upload** or **External URL**.
5. Click **Create draft**.

The policy is created as a draft. The DNS record is not published until you choose to publish it.

::warning
Only upload the **public** VMC/CMC certificate file. Never upload a private key.
::

::tip
On upload, the portal performs a basic structural check of the SVG file, but cannot guarantee that all recipients will accept it. Keep the logo simple and follow the BIMI SVG Tiny PS profile.
::

## Verify

Open the policy and click **Run verification**. The portal checks, among other things:

- That the logo URL is reachable and looks BIMI-compatible.
- That any Mark Certificate is reachable.
- That DMARC is in enforcement.
- That a published BIMI record matches the policy.

The results are shown as checks on the policy page, so you can see what might be preventing display.

::note
If DMARC is not in enforcement, a warning is shown. You can still publish the record, but the logo will likely not be displayed by major recipients until DMARC is set to `p=quarantine` or `p=reject` with `pct=100`.
::

## Publish

1. Open the policy and review the preview of the TXT record that is published at `default._bimi.<domain>`.
2. Click **Publish**.
3. If a BIMI record already exists in DNS, it is overwritten on publish.

If you later replace an uploaded file, you need to **republish** so that the live record points to the new hosted URL.

The policy page shows status at three levels: **DNS record**, **Verification** and **Display at recipients**. The portal can never guarantee the last one — that is decided by the recipient.

::

## Unpublish or delete

- **Unpublish** removes the BIMI record from DNS but keeps the policy as a draft.
- **Delete** of a published policy unpublishes it first (removing the DNS record) and then deletes the policy.

To delete a published policy you may need publish permission.

## Check the result

Use the [Lookup tool](/en/email-security/lookup-tool) to verify that the BIMI record exists at `default._bimi.<domain>` and that DMARC looks correct.
